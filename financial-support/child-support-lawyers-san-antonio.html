Understanding Child Support Guidelines
In most situations, the courts will follow a fixed percentage model when determining child support obligations. The percentage will depend on the number of children and the parent obligated to pay, and it is typically based on the parent’s “monthly net resources.” Monthly net resources refer to total income earned by the person paying child support, including any additional sources of income such as Social Security, unemployment insurance, pension, or severance pay, but it does not include state and federal taxes. In most cases, the court will also subtract any deductions such as health care and child care expenses from the parent’s gross income. Child support lawyers San Antonio can help clarify how these calculations are made and ensure that the support amount is fair based on the monthly net resources.

For example, consider a medical surgeon earning $1.8 million per year who has two children with whom he lives. Under the Texas Child Support Guidelines, he would be required to pay 25% of his net income in child support, or approximately $300,000. In many instances, this amount will increase when health insurance costs and other special circumstances are considered. Child support lawyers San Antonio are experienced in handling cases involving high-income individuals and can assist in ensuring that child support payments reflect the actual needs of the children.

However, the rules are not entirely set in stone and the courts may deviate from the guidelines if the evidence shows that it is appropriate. To support a deviation from the guidelines, the obligor parent must provide documentation of substantial and material changes in circumstances that affect the standard of support. Child support lawyers San Antonio are adept at presenting evidence that may justify a deviation from the standard guidelines, ensuring that the child support order reflects the best interests of the child.
